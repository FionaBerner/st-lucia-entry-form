# 🌦️ Plan Your Visit to St. Lucia

St. Lucia is a year‑round destination, but planning ahead helps you make the most of your trip. This guide covers weather, best times to visit, flights, money, transportation, festivals, activities, and essential emergency contacts.

## Weather and Best Time to Visit
St. Lucia has a warm tropical climate with two main seasons:
- **Dry season (December–April)** — Best weather, lower humidity, ideal for beach days and hiking.
- **Wet season (June–November)** — Higher humidity, short tropical showers, greener landscapes, and lower prices.
- **Hurricane season (June–November)** — St. Lucia is south of the main hurricane belt, but storms can still occur.

The most popular months are **December to April**, while **May and June** offer a balance of good weather and better prices.

## Flights to St. Lucia
St. Lucia has two airports:
- **Hewanorra International Airport (UVF)** — Main airport for long‑haul flights.
- **George F. L. Charles Airport (SLU)** — Regional Caribbean flights.

Direct flights commonly arrive from:
- The United States (Miami, New York, Atlanta)
- Canada (Toronto, Montreal)
- The UK (London Gatwick)
- Regional hubs (Barbados, Antigua, Trinidad)

## Money and Tipping
- **Currency:** Eastern Caribbean Dollar (XCD), but USD is widely accepted.
- **ATMs:** Available in major towns and resorts.
- **Tipping:** Common but not mandatory. Typical amounts:
  - Restaurants: 10–15% if service charge isn’t included.
  - Taxis: Round up or add 10%.
  - Hotel staff: Small daily tips for housekeeping or bell service.

## Getting Around the Island
Transportation options include:
- **Taxis** — Widely available; fares are fixed by zone.
- **Rental cars** — Good for exploring independently; driving is on the **left**.
- **Water taxis** — Scenic and fast for beach‑to‑beach travel.
- **Minibuses** — Cheapest option, used mostly by locals.
- **Private drivers** — Popular for day trips and airport transfers.

## Festivals and Events (Month by Month)
- **January:** New Year’s celebrations, sailing events.
- **February:** Independence Day festivities.
- **March–April:** Easter cultural events.
- **May:** St. Lucia Jazz & Arts Festival.
- **June–July:** Carnival season.
- **August:** Emancipation Day events.
- **October:** Creole Heritage Month.
- **December:** Christmas festivals and boat parades.

## Popular Activities
- Hiking the **Gros Piton** or **Petit Piton**.
- Snorkeling and diving in marine reserves.
- Sulphur Springs drive‑in volcano.
- Botanical gardens and waterfalls.
- Catamaran cruises and sunset sails.
- Chocolate plantation tours.
- Ziplining and rainforest adventures.

## Emergency Phone Numbers
- **Police:** 999 or 911  
- **Fire:** 911  
- **Ambulance:** 911  
- **Marine Rescue:** Coast Guard via 911  

## Important Reminder
Foreign visitors must complete the [**mandatory St. Lucia Entry Form**](https://stluciaentryform.com/) before traveling. This online form is required for smooth, visa‑free entry into the country.
